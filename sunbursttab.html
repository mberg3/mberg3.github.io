The Ionizing Stellar Continuum of the Sunburst Arc

The Sunburst Arc is a brightly-lensed galaxy at z~2.37 and is a confirmed Lyman continuum leaker. Using HST/WFC3 UVIS G280 slitless grism spectroscopy, I am comparing models to observations of the ionizing stellar continuum of a galaxy for the first time. I am also measuring the escape fractions and interstellar medium and intergalactic medium transmissivities.

This is the HST/ACS image of the Sunburst Arc. It is lensed into four arc segments by a foreground galaxy cluster. Incredibly, the leaking region of this galaxy has been images 12 times over all four arcs.
